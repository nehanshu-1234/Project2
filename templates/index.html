What is Mindfulness

Mindfulness. It’s a pretty straightforward word. It suggests that the mind is fully attending to what’s happening, to what you’re doing, to the space you’re moving through. That might seem trivial, except for the annoying fact that we so often veer from the matter at hand. Our mind takes flight, we lose touch with our body, and pretty soon we’re engrossed in obsessive thoughts about something that just happened or fretting about the future. And that makes us anxious. Mindfulness is the basic human ability to be fully present, aware of where we are and what we’re doing, and not overly reactive or overwhelmed by what’s going on around us.

8 limbs of yoga

Yama

Yama (Restraints): The ethical principles governing one's behavior. They include Ahimsa (non-violence), Satya (truthfulness), Asteya (non-stealing), Brahmacharya (celibacy or right use of energy), and Aparigraha (non-possessiveness).

Niyama

Niyama (Observances): Personal observances for self-discipline and spiritual growth. They include Saucha (cleanliness), Santosha (contentment), Tapas (discipline), Svadhyaya (self-study), and Ishvara Pranidhana (surrender to a higher power).

Asanas

Asana (Postures): The physical practice of yoga postures to attain physical health, steadiness, and comfort for meditation. Asanas are designed to prepare the body for prolonged meditation.

Pranayama

Pranayama (Breath Control): The regulation and extension of breath. Pranayama practices enhance the flow of life force energy (prana) and help prepare the mind for meditation.

Pratyahara

Pratyahara (Withdrawal of Senses): The conscious withdrawal of the senses from external stimuli, turning attention inward. This stage prepares the mind for deeper concentration and meditation

Dharana

Dharana (Concentration): Focused attention on a single point or object. Concentration helps the mind become steady and one-pointed, paving the way for meditation.

Dhyana

Dhyana (Meditation): Sustained concentration leads to meditation. In this stage, the meditator experiences a continuous flow of awareness without distraction.

Samadhi

Samadhi (Union): The ultimate goal of yoga, where the meditator achieves a state of oneness with the object of meditation, experiencing pure consciousness. Samadhi is a state of profound joy, peace, and spiritual realization.
